About

Discover the wonders of snakes

  • Anaconda

    The Largest Snake in the World

    The green anaconda is the largest snake in the world, capable of growing up to 30 feet long and weighing over 500 pounds. They are powerful swimmers and can hold their breath for up to 10 minutes while submerged in water.

  • Cobra

    The Venomous Snake

    The cobra is one of the most venomous snakes in the world. Their venom can cause paralysis and even death, but they also play an important role in their ecosystems by controlling rodent populations.

  • Python

    The Constrictor Snake

    Pythons are non-venomous snakes that use constriction to subdue their prey. They are capable of swallowing prey that is much larger than their own bodies, such as deer and antelopes.

  • Rattlesnake

    The Noisy Snake

    Rattlesnakes are named for the rattle on the end of their tail, which they use to warn predators and humans to stay away. They are venomous and can be found in various habitats across North and South America.

  • Snakes as Pets

    Low Maintenance and Fascinating

    Snakes make excellent pets for those who are looking for a low-maintenance and unique companion. They require minimal space, feeding, and attention, and can be fascinating to watch as they move and explore their environment.

  • Benefit to Ecosystem

    Regulating Pest Populations

    Snakes play an important role in regulating pest populations in their ecosystems. They feed on rodents, insects, and other small animals that can be harmful to crops and gardens, making them valuable contributors to the balance of nature.

  • Medical Benefits

    Potential for New Medicines

    The venom of certain snake species has been found to have medicinal properties and potential for use in developing new drugs. Research is ongoing to discover and harness the potential of snake venom in the treatment of various medical conditions.

Pied Ball Python

Rarity:
The pied ball python color morph is extremely rare in the wild

The Pied Ball Python is a popular pet snake known for its striking coloration and docile temperament. These snakes have a unique pied pattern of white and dark-colored patches that create a striking contrast. They typically grow to be around 3-5 feet long and require a terrarium with a temperature range of 78-88°F and a humidity level of 50-60%. They are carnivorous and primarily eat mice or rats. As a relatively easy-to-care-for species, the Pied Ball Python is an excellent choice for first-time snake owners. Pied ball pythons are carnivorous and their diet mainly consists of small mammals such as mice or rats. It's important to provide them with pre-killed or frozen-thawed rodents to avoid injury to both the snake and the prey. The size of the food should be appropriate to the size of the snake, and they should be fed every 7 to 10 days. It's also recommended to switch up their diet with other small mammals to ensure they receive a balanced nutrition.

  • Scientific Name: Python regius
  • Common Name: Pied Ball Python
  • Appearance: Strikingly beautiful with unique pattern.
  • Temperament: Generally docile, great for beginners.
  • Feeding: Carnivore, eats rodents regularly.
  • Habitat: Warm and humid terrarium needed.
  • Lifespan: Can live up to 30 years.
  • Price: $299
